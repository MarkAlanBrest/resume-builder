Main Campus — New Castle, PA

Program Name
Automotive Technology — Main Campus (New Castle, PA)
Credential Earned
Associate in Specialized Technology

Program Overview (Resume-Safe)
The Automotive Technology program prepares students for entry-level automotive technician roles. Training emphasizes diagnostics, repair procedures, and modern vehicle systems through a combination of classroom instruction and extensive hands-on lab experience.

Primary Technical Training Areas
Students receive instruction and hands-on exposure in the following areas:
* Engine repair and engine performance
* Brake systems
* Suspension and steering systems
* Electrical and electronic systems
* HVAC systems
* Drivetrain systems
* Computerized vehicle diagnostics

Hands-On & Lab Experience
As part of the program, students typically participate in lab-based activities involving:
* Vehicle inspection and preventive maintenance procedures
* Diagnostic testing using scan tools and meters
* Mechanical repair and component replacement
* Electrical system troubleshooting
* HVAC service procedures
* Brake, suspension, and steering service
Include only experiences confirmed by the student.

Automotive Technical Skills Developed
Applicable resume skills may include:
* Automotive diagnostics
* Engine and drivetrain service
* Brake system inspection and repair
* Suspension and steering service
* Electrical troubleshooting
* HVAC service and recovery
* OBD II scanning and fault code analysis
* Preventive maintenance
* Battery, charging, and starting systems

Tools & Equipment Exposure
Students may gain experience using industry-standard tools such as:
* OBD II scan tools
* Multimeters
* Vehicle lifts and jacks
* Torque wrenches
* A/C recovery and recharge machines
* Brake service equipment
Include tools only if used by the student.
