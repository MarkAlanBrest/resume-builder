Program Overview (Resume-Safe)
The Electrical & Industrial Maintenance program prepares students for entry-level roles in industrial electrical and maintenance environments. Training emphasizes electrical systems, mechanical maintenance, motors and controls, troubleshooting, preventive maintenance, and industrial safety through hands-on lab experience.

Primary Technical Training Areas
Students receive instruction and hands-on exposure in:
* Industrial electrical systems
* Mechanical maintenance fundamentals
* Electric motors and basic motor controls
* Troubleshooting electrical and mechanical systems
* Preventive maintenance procedures
* Industrial safety practices

Hands-On & Lab Experience
As part of the program, students typically participate in lab-based activities involving:
* Troubleshooting electrical faults in industrial training systems
* Inspecting and performing basic maintenance on electric motors
* Servicing mechanical components and equipment
* Performing preventive maintenance tasks
* Applying lockout/tagout procedures during maintenance activities
* Following industrial safety standards
Include only experiences confirmed by the student.
