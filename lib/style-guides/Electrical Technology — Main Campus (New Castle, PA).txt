Program Overview (Resume-Safe)
The Electrical Technology program prepares students for entry-level positions in residential, commercial, and industrial electrical fields. Training emphasizes electrical installation, motors and controls, blueprint reading, troubleshooting, and electrical code awareness through classroom instruction and hands-on lab experience.

Primary Technical Training Areas
Students receive instruction and hands-on exposure in:
* Residential electrical wiring
* Commercial electrical systems
* Industrial electrical fundamentals
* Motors and motor control systems
* Blueprint and electrical diagram interpretation
* Electrical codes and standards (NEC awareness)

Hands-On & Lab Experience
As part of the program, students typically participate in lab-based activities involving:
* Installing residential and commercial wiring systems
* Bending and installing conduit
* Wiring basic motor control circuits
* Installing electrical panels and components
* Troubleshooting electrical circuits and faults
* Applying electrical safety practices and code guidelines
Include only experiences confirmed by the student.
