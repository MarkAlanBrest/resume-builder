Program Overview (Resume-Safe)
The Electro Mechanical Technology program prepares students for entry-level maintenance technician and industrial systems roles. Training combines electrical, mechanical, and automation fundamentals with an emphasis on troubleshooting, equipment maintenance, and preventive maintenance through hands-on lab experience.

Primary Technical Training Areas
Students receive instruction and hands-on exposure in:
* Electrical systems fundamentals
* Mechanical systems and components
* Programmable Logic Controllers (PLCs)
* Automation systems
* Motors and motor drives
* Electrical and mechanical troubleshooting

Hands-On & Lab Experience
As part of the program, students typically participate in lab-based activities involving:
* Diagnosing electrical and mechanical faults
* Working with PLC simulations and training systems
* Performing maintenance tasks on industrial equipment
* Installing and servicing motors and drives
* Applying preventive maintenance procedures
* Interpreting electrical and mechanical schematics
Include only experiences confirmed by the student.
