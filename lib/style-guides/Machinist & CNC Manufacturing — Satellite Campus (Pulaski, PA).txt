Program Overview (Resume-Safe)
The CNC Machining & Manufacturing Technology program prepares students for entry-level machining and manufacturing roles. Training emphasizes CNC machining, manual machining, blueprint reading, precision measurement, and basic CNC programming through hands-on lab experience with mills and lathes.

Primary Technical Training Areas
Students receive instruction and hands-on exposure in:
* CNC programming fundamentals (G-code)
* CNC machine setup and operation
* CNC milling and turning operations
* Manual machining fundamentals
* Blueprint reading and interpretation
* Geometric Dimensioning & Tolerancing (GD&T) awareness
* Precision measurement techniques
* Tooling and workholding concepts

Hands-On & Lab Experience
As part of the program, students typically participate in lab-based activities involving:
* Setting up CNC machines and tooling
* Writing, editing, and running basic G-code programs
* Operating CNC mills and lathes
* Producing machined parts to specified tolerances
* Measuring and inspecting parts using precision instruments
* Interpreting blueprints and GD&T symbols
* Performing basic machine maintenance tasks
Include only experiences confirmed by the student.
