Program Overview (Resume-Safe)
The Machinist & CNC Manufacturing program prepares students for entry-level machinist and CNC manufacturing roles. Training emphasizes manual machining, CNC setup and operation, blueprint reading, precision measurement, and basic CNC programming through hands-on lab experience.

Primary Technical Training Areas
Students receive instruction and hands-on exposure in:
* Manual machining operations
* CNC machining fundamentals
* CNC machine setup and operation
* Blueprint and shop drawing interpretation
* Precision measurement techniques
* Manufacturing processes and shop practices

Hands-On & Lab Experience
As part of the program, students typically participate in lab-based activities involving:
* Machining parts using manual milling and lathe equipment
* Setting up and operating CNC mills and lathes
* Producing components to specification
* Measuring parts to tolerance using precision instruments
* Interpreting blueprints and shop drawings for part production
* Following manufacturing safety and quality practices
Include only experiences confirmed by the student.
