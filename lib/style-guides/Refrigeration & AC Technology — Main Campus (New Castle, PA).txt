PROGRAM INFORMATION — HVAC TECHNOLOGY
Main Campus — New Castle, PA

Program Name
HVAC Technology — Main Campus (New Castle, PA)
Credential Earned
Associate in Specialized Technology

Program Overview (Resume-Safe)
The HVAC Technology program prepares students for entry-level roles in heating, ventilation, air conditioning, and refrigeration. Training emphasizes system installation, diagnostics, service procedures, electrical troubleshooting, and preventive maintenance through hands-on lab experience.

Primary Technical Training Areas
Students receive instruction and hands-on exposure in:
* Heating systems
* Cooling and air conditioning systems
* Refrigeration fundamentals
* Electrical components and controls
* Airflow and system performance
* HVAC diagnostics

Hands-On & Lab Experience
As part of the program, students typically participate in lab-based activities involving:
* Installing HVAC components and systems
* Diagnosing heating and cooling system issues
* Servicing refrigeration systems
* Performing electrical troubleshooting on HVAC equipment
* Measuring airflow and system performance
* Conducting preventive maintenance tasks
* Following safety and environmental guidelines
Include only experiences confirmed by the student.

HVAC Skills Developed
Applicable resume skills may include:
* HVAC system installation
* HVAC diagnostics and troubleshooting
* Refrigeration service fundamentals
* Electrical troubleshooting
* Airflow measurement and system balancing
* Preventive maintenance procedures
* Safety and compliance awareness

Tools & Equipment Exposure
Students may gain experience using HVAC service tools such as:
* Manifold gauge sets
* Multimeters
* Refrigerant recovery machines
* Thermometers and temperature probes
* Hand tools
Include tools only if used by the student.

Project & Lab Experience Examples
When appropriate, resumes may reference general program-based lab work such as:
